Internal Comms System · Gov IT · Moscow · 2020–2023
Building an Internal Comms Ecosystem for 3,500+ Employees
The Challenge

The Dept. of IT, Moscow Government had no unified internal communications system. With 3,500+ employees spread across product teams, no shared content logic, and no engagement culture — the team was informed but not connected. Communications were reactive: evacuation notices, not strategy.

My Role

Member of the core Corporate Communications team. Responsible for content strategy, editorial production, the weekly digest, and communication scripts for campaigns and events. Worked within a 12-person team that collectively owned all 9 communication channels.

What We Built

A full-scale internal media and engagement system: 9 content rubrics (employee stories, "how it works" explainers, leadership Q&As, career profiles, team life, achievements), 9 communication channels (portal articles, email campaigns, weekly digest, messenger posts, banners, events, corporate TV, intranet landings, pulse surveys), editorial policy, and a content plan with segmented audience targeting and analytics cycles.

Corporate Media · Healthcare · 2018–2020
Corporate Newspaper & Employer Brand, SM-Clinic Group
The Challenge

SM-Clinic is one of Russia's largest private healthcare groups, with clinics across multiple cities. The internal brand was fragmented — staff didn't feel connected across locations, and the company had no consistent voice for employer storytelling. There was a corporate newspaper in name only, with minimal readership.

What I Did

Took full ownership of internal corporate media: relaunched the newspaper with a new editorial concept, wrote and edited interviews with doctors and managers, produced culture stories and employer brand content. Launched and ran the company's employer Instagram from scratch, growing it to 900+ followers. Also led engagement campaigns and internal communications for all-staff announcements.

Employer Brand · Finance · Dubai · EN · 2024–2025
Employer Brand & Tone of Voice, Skybound Wealth Management
The Challenge

Skybound is a Dubai-based international wealth management firm with a distributed team across multiple countries. Despite strong business positioning externally, the employer brand lacked consistency — different team leads communicated in different tones, and there was no unified internal narrative or content framework.

What I Did

As a contract consultant, I owned the internal communications and employer brand function end-to-end. Built a unified tone of voice guide, developed content frameworks for leadership and HR communications, and wrote expert articles in English for the company's external channels — including pieces on expat financial planning and women in investing. Partnered with HR and leadership to align culture messaging with business priorities.
