Internal Comms System · Gov IT · Moscow · 2020–2023
Building an Internal Comms Ecosystem for 3,500+ Employees
The Challenge

The Dept. of IT, Moscow Government had no unified internal communications system. With 3,500+ employees spread across product teams, no shared content logic, and no engagement culture — the team was informed but not connected. Communications were reactive: evacuation notices, not strategy.

My Role

Member of the core Corporate Communications team. Responsible for content strategy, editorial production, the weekly digest, and communication scripts for campaigns and events. Worked within a 12-person team that collectively owned all 9 communication channels.

What We Built

A full-scale internal media and engagement system: 9 content rubrics (employee stories, "how it works" explainers, leadership Q&As, career profiles, team life, achievements), 9 communication channels (portal articles, email campaigns, weekly digest, messenger posts, banners, events, corporate TV, intranet landings, pulse surveys), editorial policy, and a content plan with segmented audience targeting and analytics cycles.

21 Non-Standard Internal Communication Channels That Engage Employees Far More Powerfully Than Email
Guide
+

Most companies use email as their primary internal channel — and wonder why nobody reads it. This guide maps 21 non-obvious touchpoints where employees are already present and receptive: from elevator screens to Wi-Fi push notifications, CEO voice messages to printer displays.

  • Physical environment channels — TV screens, wall projections, bathroom mirrors, coffee cups
  • Micro-touchpoints — Wi-Fi notifications, printer screens, QR codes on meeting room doors
  • Audio formats — CEO voice messages, internal podcasts, audio news digests
  • Gamification — interactive quizzes, Telegram bots, live leaderboards
  • Behavioural triggers — staircase messages, pop-up cards, desktop easter eggs

Based on real cases from the Dept. of IT, Moscow Government and SM-Clinic Group.

Why Your Corporate Portal Has No Traffic — And How to Fix It
Guide
+

A corporate intranet with no readers is just an expensive folder. The problem is almost never technical — it's editorial, structural, and cultural. This guide walks through the most common reasons portals fail and what to do about each one.

  • Wrong content architecture — organised for the company, not the employee
  • No editorial ownership — nobody responsible for what gets published
  • Missing adoption strategy — launched with a bang, forgotten in a week
  • No feedback loops — you publish but never learn what people actually need

What Is Editorial Policy and Why Every Internal Media Needs One
Article
+

Without editorial policy, internal communications drift — different people write in different tones, topics get repeated or missed, and the whole thing feels inconsistent. An editorial policy is the document that prevents all of that. Here's what it includes and how to build one.

  • Tone of voice — how you sound, what you avoid, what you never say
  • Content rubrics — recurring formats that give structure to your editorial calendar
  • Approval workflow — who reviews what before it goes out
  • Channel guidelines — what content belongs where
